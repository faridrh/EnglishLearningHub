### English Level Placement Test

**Instructions**: Choose the best answer (A, B, C, or D) for each question. There is only one correct answer per question. Good luck!

#### Section 1: Grammar
1. What is the correct form of the verb in this sentence?  
   "She ___ to the store every Saturday."  
   A) go  
   B) goes  
   C) going  
   D) is going  

2. Choose the correct pronoun:  
   "This book belongs to ___. I lent it to her yesterday."  
   A) I  
   B) me  
   C) my  
   D) mine  

3. Which sentence is correct?  
   A) I don’t have no money.  
   B) I don’t have any money.  
   C) I doesn’t have any money.  
   D) I no have any money.  

4. What is the correct past tense of the verb "to buy"?  
   A) Buyed  
   B) Bought  
   C) Buying  
   D) Buys  

5. Complete the sentence:  
   "If I ___ enough money, I would travel the world."  
   A) have  
   B) had  
   C) has  
   D) having  

6. Which is the correct question form?  
   A) Where you live?  
   B) Where do you live?  
   C) Where you do live?  
   D) Where does you live?  

7. Choose the correct word:  
   "There are ___ people waiting outside."  
   A) much  
   B) many  
   C) a lot  
   D) any  

8. Which sentence uses the correct conditional?  
   A) If I will study, I pass the exam.  
   B) If I study, I will pass the exam.  
   C) If I studied, I pass the exam.  
   D) If I studying, I will pass the exam.

### Scoring Guide
- **0–8 correct**: Beginner (A1–A2)  
  The test-taker has basic knowledge and needs to focus on fundamental grammar, vocabulary, and sentence structure.  
- **9–16 correct**: Intermediate (B1–B2)  
  The test-taker has a good grasp of English but may need to work on more complex grammar and vocabulary.  
- **17–22 correct**: Upper-Intermediate (B2–C1)  
  The test-taker is proficient but may make occasional errors in advanced structures or nuanced vocabulary.  
- **23–25 correct**: Advanced (C1–C2)  
  The test-taker has a strong command of English and can handle complex language tasks.
