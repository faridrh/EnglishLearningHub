### Quick Review Questions

### Articles
**Choose the correct article:**
1. I saw ___ elephant at the zoo.
   - a) a
   - b) an
   - c) the
   
2. ___ sun is very bright today.
   - a) A
   - b) An
   - c) The

**Answers:** 1-b, 2-c

---

### Plural Nouns
**Write the plural form:**
1. book → ______
2. child → ______
3. woman → ______

**Answers:** 1-books, 2-children, 3-women

---

### Present Simple
**Complete with the correct form:**
1. She _____ (work) in a hospital.
2. They _____ (not like) coffee.
3. _____ you speak English?

**Answers:** 1-works, 2-don't like, 3-Do

---

### Present Continuous
**Choose the correct form:**
1. Right now, I _____ my homework.
   - a) do
   - b) am doing
   - c) does

2. The children _____ in the garden.
   - a) are playing
   - b) is playing
   - c) play

**Answers:** 1-b, 2-a

---

### Subject Pronouns
**Replace with the correct pronoun:**
1. John is tall. _____ is my friend.
2. Mary and I are students. _____ study English.
3. The book is interesting. _____ is on the table.

**Answers:** 1-He, 2-We, 3-It

---

### Possessive Adjectives
**Complete with my, your, his, her, its, our, their:**
1. This is _____ car. (belonging to me)
2. Where is _____ bag? (belonging to you)
3. The cat is cleaning _____ fur.

**Answers:** 1-my, 2-your, 3-its

### Quick Review Questions

### Past Perfect
**Complete with past perfect:**
1. When I arrived, she _____ already _____ (leave).
2. They _____ never _____ (see) such a beautiful sunset.

**Answers:** 1-had left, 2-had seen

---

### Modal Verbs
**Choose the correct modal:**
1. You _____ wear a helmet when riding a bike.
   - a) can
   - b) must
   - c) might

2. It _____ rain tomorrow.
   - a) should
   - b) must
   - c) might

**Answers:** 1-b, 2-c

---

### Passive Voice
**Change to passive voice:**
1. The teacher explains the lesson.
   → The lesson _____ by the teacher.
2. Someone stole my bicycle.
   → My bicycle _____.

**Answers:** 1-is explained, 2-was stolen

---

### Conditionals 1 & 2
**Complete the conditional:**
1. If it _____ (rain), I _____ (stay) home. (Type 1)
2. If I _____ (be) rich, I _____ (travel) the world. (Type 2)

**Answers:** 1-rains, will stay  2-were, would travel

---

### Relative Clauses
**Combine using who, which, that:**
1. The man lives next door. He is a doctor.
   → The man _____ lives next door is a doctor.
2. I bought a book. It was very expensive.
   → The book _____ I bought was very expensive.

**Answers:** 1-who, 2-that/which

---

### Reported Speech
**Change to reported speech:**
1. "I am tired," she said.
   → She said _____.
2. "Where do you live?" he asked.
   → He asked _____.

**Answers:** 1-that she was tired, 2-where I lived

### Quick Review Questions

### Subjunctive Mood
**Complete with subjunctive:**
1. I suggest that he _____ (go) to the doctor.
2. It's important that she _____ (be) on time.

**Answers:** 1-go, 2-be

---

### Inversion
**Rewrite using inversion:**
1. I have never seen such a beautiful sunset.
   → Never _____ such a beautiful sunset.
2. She not only sings but also dances.
   → Not only _____ but she also dances.

**Answers:** 1-have I seen, 2-does she sing

---

### Cleft Sentences
**Rewrite as cleft sentence:**
1. John bought the car yesterday.
   → It was _____ who bought the car yesterday.
2. I need your help.
   → What _____ is your help.

**Answers:** 1-John, 2-I need

---

### Conditionals 3 & Mixed
**Complete the conditional:**
1. If she _____ (study) harder, she _____ (pass) the exam. (Type 3)
2. If I were you, I _____ (accept) that job offer yesterday. (Mixed)

**Answers:** 1-had studied, would have passed  2-would have accepted

---

### Ellipsis & Substitution
**Complete with ellipsis or substitution:**
1. "Are you coming to the party?" "I hope _____."
2. My car is faster than _____ (your car).

**Answers:** 1-so, 2-yours

---

### Participle Clauses
**Rewrite using participle clause:**
1. Because she was tired, she went to bed early.
   → _____ tired, she went to bed early.
2. The man who is standing there is my teacher.
   → The man _____ there is my teacher.

**Answers:** 1-Being, 2-standing
